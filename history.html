  • 1997

    The district was one of five districts in New York State where more students were enrolled in private school than in public school due to religious reasons. Of the 18,000 children transported by the district, 10,500 were students of yeshiva private schools.

  • 2005

    the Orthodox Jewish population of the district gained majority control of the school board. This new board began reducing the budget and lowering taxes. There was a prioritization of the private school Yeshiva students

  • 2010

    due to lowered taxes and reduced budgets, the district's finances had decreased and services were reduced at the schools, forcing students to take five- and six-year graduation plans instead of four-year plans. Schools were heavily understaffed.

  • 2011

    After an audit, the districvtbwas exposed for gross mismanagement of funds. the audit found that District officials inaccurately projected a June 30, 2010 fund balance of $13 million when preparing District's 2010-11 austerity budget that was presented to the electorate and on which cutbacks and layoffs were based.

  • 2011-2012

    During this school year, 9 out of the 14 public schools in the district were deemed "schools in need of improvement" by the nys department of ed

  • April, 2014

    Rockland Clergy for social justice, pleaded to Governor Cuomo to intervene

  • November, 2014

    In November 2014 a state-appointed monitor charged with investigating the district delivered a sharply critical assessment. The monitor, Hank Greenberg, said the board showed favoritism to Jewish students who attend private Orthodox schools in the district, and cut services to the 9,000 district students, mostly black and Hispanic, who attend the district's public schools.

  • 2015

    Dr.Joel Klein resigns as the superintendent of East Ramapo, After being under fire due to racist, antiimmigrant remarks he made regarding the districts growing hispanic population.
