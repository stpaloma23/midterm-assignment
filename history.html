A Brief History of East Ramapo

  • 1997

    The district was one of five districts in New York State where more students were enrolled in private school than in public school due to religious reasons. Of the 18,000 children transported by the district, 10,500 were students of private schools.

  • 2005

    The Orthodox Jewish (Private school) population of the district gained majority control of the school board. This new board began reducing the budget and lowering taxes. There was a prioritization of the private school Yeshiva students

  • 2010

    Due to low taxes and reduced budgets, the district's finances had decreased and services were reduced at the schools, forcing students to take five- and six-year graduation plans instead of four-year plans. Schools were heavily understaffed.

  • 2011

    After an audit, the district was exposed for gross mismanagement of funds. the audit found that district officials inaccurately projected a June 30, 2010 fund balance of $13 million when preparing the district's 2010-11 budget that was presented to the electorate and was what cutbacks and layoffs were based off of.

  • 2011-2012

    During this school year, 9 out of the 14 public schools in the district were deemed "schools in need of improvement" by the NYS Department of Education

  • April, 2014

    Rockland Clergy for social justice, pleaded to Governor Cuomo to intervene

  • November, 2014

    In November 2014 a state-appointed monitor incharge of supervising the district, Hank Greenberg, said the board showed favoritism to the private schools in the district, and cut services to the 9,000 public school students, mostly black and Hispanic.

  • 2015

    Superintendent Dr.Joel Klein resigns as the superintendent of East Ramapo, after being under fire due to racist, anti-immigrant remarks he made regarding the districts' growing hispanic population.

  • 2016

    A new Superintendent is appointed: Dr. Deborah L. Wortham. She is appointed because of her previous ability to convert failing school districts and instill condifence in the students.

  • 2017

    The National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) filed a lawsuit sueing the district. Claiming that majority of the board members being Orthodox Jewish men who did not participate in the public school system, was putting the black and brown students who attend the public schools at a disadvantage.

  • March 2020

    The coronairus pandemic occurs, forcing a district where 75% of students are on free or reduced lunch, and majority of students are low income to participate in remote learning. Due to poor planning, large portions of students were unable to get access to chrome books and internet access.

  • June 2020

    The NAACP wins the lawsuit against the district. Forcing the district to participate in the ward system for electing school board members. The court sided with the NAACP agreeing that the majority Orthodox presence on the school board lead to the black and brown students of the district not being prioritiezd.

  • June 2020

    Dr.Wortham leaves the district and a new Interim Superintendent is appointed Dr. Ray Giamartino By the time of her departure, All of the schools except the highschools were in good academic standing. When she came, the district was being threated to be taken over the New York State

  • January 2021

    The Interim Super Intendent claims the NAACP lawsuit fees caused a 4.3 million dollar deficit. He then proceeded to announce a series of budget cuts, one that consisted of firing 32 teachers and increasing class sizes.

  • February 2021

    The first election using the ward system is held. Three representatives of the public school community was placed on the board.

  • March 2021

    After a lot of outrage and negative press about the pojected budget cuts, the Superintendent claims that he will only fire 3 full time employees. The fight continues for the East Ramapo Central School District.
